HexaPlanter

raised mobile planter bed

HEXAPLANTER

is a raised planter bed that grants better access to older people, when watering and tending to plants, because it provides multiple heights of plant beds that can be worked on without the user having to bend over. The lazy susan paired with the handles that go around the entire planter allow for easy turning and accessibility to all sides of the hexagon. The planter can be indoors during winter and outdoors during summer making the planter accessible year round. The large wheels make it easy to move the planter in the uneven outdoor surfaces of Westwood and have locks for safety. The planter is also painted with bright, fun colors to help bring cheer to the residentsâ€™ lives. This project was created for Design Thinking and Communication, a 10-week engineering course at Northwestern University.

my role

I was the project manager and responsible for overseeing our progress within our timeline and delegating individual assignments along with overseeing our final report. As a team member, I participated in user research, prototype testing, and built and painted wooden components.

building a prototype

In order to create a working prototype within the short project timeline, we used materials already found in the Ford Design Center. From this we were able to engineer a wooden base and top and details for the mechanics. Our final prototype measured 3'x3'x3.5'.
