KEYSHOT

A Personal Project

is a rendering program that I learned for a Northwestern class.

Our final assignment was to choose a model of any product and produce two renderings of it using KeyShot and Adobe Photoshop for post-editing. I decided to model a chair due to the commonality of the product, since I thought it would be easier to judge the quality of my renderings by how believable they are. I produced two images- one with the chair in a studio environment with minimal background, and the other with the chair in a home environment with more background noise in order to explore different environments and lighting solutions.

FINAL RENDERINGS

process

Since this project was very open-ended I was able to determine many parameters myself. My goal was to create a believable model that could be placed in two very different environments in order to show mastery of the Keyshot program. I was inspired by this rendering by keyshot influencer Esben Oxholm, and wanted especially to replicate the believability of the materials.
