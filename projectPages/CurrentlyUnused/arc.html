ARCSTART

Summer Architecture Exploration

is where I realized my passion for design. Formally, it is a 3 week long summer program for High School Students at the University of Michigan, where students can experience an actual architecture studio class. However, to me it was so much more. Growing up I have always been creative and enjoyed the arts, but it was always just a hobby. This program showed me the beauty of design and that I could be creative in a career, which led me to where I am today.

All of the projects taught me an immense amount, but the final project was a culmination of all I had learned in this program. The final project of the program was to independently design a large building whose form was based on action words. Through sketching and small cardboard models I created a form that I believed truly embraced the two verbs I chose- “slice” and “taper”. Since this was a summer program, I decided the function of the building after the form when saw that the shape lended itself to a hotel building.

The final model

Built at 1/8th scale, my final model made from 4 ply and 2 ply museum board reached over 2ft in height.

finding inspiration

To create the form of my hotel we were first given multiple action words that we had to translate into models. Examples of such words include "intersect", "taper", "slice", and "branch".

deciding on a shape

From my inital sketches and carboard mockups, I liked my ideas that stemed from the words "slice" and "taper". I combined those two words to create the model on the left and then refined my idea to create the model on the right, which served as my final design prototype.

building the model

The largest segments of my model were made of 4 ply museum board in order to be sturdy enough to support the rest of the model, which was made of 2 ply museum board. My final sketches were hand drawn at 1/16th scale with HB pencil on bristol paper.
