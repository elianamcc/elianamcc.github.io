Capstone

Northwestern Senior Capstone

For my senior capstone, I worked on developing a design for an instant hot water heater catered toward coffee users.

I had an amazing experience working on this project throughout my senior year. The project's goal was to design a high fidelity works-like looks-like prototype for a need that users experience on a regular basis. Our teams were able to find these needs on our own, but we had to ensure our final prototype was designed for manufacturability and had a viable commercialization strategy. While this project is still ongoing, I have broadened my team management skills, completed extensive user research and have gained engineering skills through technical testing.

Moving Forward

Final Goal

Our ultimate goal for the class is to create a high fidelity works-like looks-like prototype. This prototype should be designed for manufacturability and we need to create a commercialization strategy.

Current Steps

Currently our team is looking at producing a prototype that will acomplish our desired metrics. To do this we are in the middle of testing to find the best internal components for our use case. Once those are selected, we will ensure our product architecture accomadates those specific parts. We are also considering sustainability and lean design while we are creating our product as another point of differentiation. These points of differentiation will be key in our commercialization strategy. Along with these steps, we are performing a patent claims analysis to start the process of applying for a provisional patent for our product.
